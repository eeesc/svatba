Venues

The ceremony will be at the Church of St. Bartholomew in Velká Chmelištná. The reception is at the campsite in Soseň. You can sleep in four-bed cabins with bunk beds, in rooms, on the floor of the common room, in your own tent, or under the stars. There are fewer beds in rooms and cabins than there are guests — please tell us your preferences in the RSVP form below. If you would rather sleep away from the wedding hubbub in a quieter spot, you can book a guesthouse in nearby Jesenice.

Travel

Getting there

The village is not far from Prague, but public transport is limited. We are arranging a coach from Rakovník leaving at 11:00 (connecting with R24 trains: 9:00 from Masaryk station and line T7, 9:07 from the main station). If you are driving, please let us know in advance and offer spare seats if you can.

Group bike ride

On Friday, June 12, we are organising a group ride from Prague. You can join in Prague or along the way — for example in Rakovník. Tick the box in the form if you are interested and we will follow up.

Parking — Chmelištná

You can park by the crossroads in the village, near the municipal office, and in the garden behind our cottage. Everything will be signed on the day.

Parking — Soseň

You can park in the campsite, but space is limited. Please register your car in advance. If the car park is full, you can park in Velká Chmelištná.

Between venues

The ceremony and the party are in different places. A big part of the day is the walk together, but there will also be a shuttle that can carry luggage or give you a lift if you need it.

Leaving

We need to vacate the campsite by 1:00 p.m. There is no organised group transport back; from Oráčov station (3 km on foot) trains to Prague leave at 8:46, 9:33, 10:45 (direct), 11:32, 12:42, 13:34, 14:45, 15:31 and later.

What to bring

  • Dressy but comfortable clothes: the ceremony is in church and most of the celebration is outdoors. Wear something that feels good, easy, and appropriately festive — think forest casual.
  • Sturdy, comfortable shoes for a five-kilometre walk.
  • If you play an instrument, bring it — we will light a fire at the campsite and there will be time to play and sing (Hudson River shanties especially welcome).
  • We would love it if you brought a favourite wedding photo (yours, family, or friends). We hope to display them outside — please bring a copy in case of weather damage.
  • Sleeping bag and mat if you are sleeping in a tent, under the stars, or on the floor of the common room.
